What Is a Cookie?

A cookie is a small file of letters and numbers that we store on your browser or the hard drive of your device, which can be used in combination with pixel tags, local storage objects, and similar technologies.

What Cookies Do We Use and Why?

Cookies you may receive while visiting our website

  1. When you visit our website located at www.adswizz.com, we use cookies (collectively, “ADSWIZZ WEBSITE COOKIES” unless otherwise noted) to distinguish you from other users of the website. This helps us to provide you with a good experience when you browse the website and also allows us to improve it.

All the ADSWIZZ WEBSITE COOKIES we use are Strictly Necessary in that such cookies let you move around the website and use essential features of the website or features that you specifically request to use. We use these Strictly Necessary cookies to e.g.:

  • Make sure you connect to the right service on the website when we make any changes to the way it works.
  • For security purposes, in particular the login areas and in order to remember that you are logged in.
  • Analyze if some users experience issues getting the website contents.
  • Remember which pages have already been visited / which form has already been pre-filled.

Without these Strictly Necessary cookies, we may not be able to provide a seamless experience on our website or specific functionality. If you prevent these cookies, we cannot guarantee how the website or the security on the website will perform during your visit.

Although we do not need your consent to set Strictly Necessary cookies, you can nevertheless block or delete such cookies by applying specific settings to your preferred web browser. You can find more information on how to block or delete cookies in the How to Manage our Cookies section below.

The ADSWIZZ WEBISTE COOKIES we use when you visit our website can be session cookies and / or persistent cookies and / or third-party cookies as per the definitions below.

  • Persistent cookies: these cookies remain on a user’s device for the period of time specified in the cookie. They are activated each time that the user visits the website that created that particular cookie.
  • Session cookies: these cookies allow website operators to link the actions of a user during a browser session. A browser session starts when a user opens the browser window and finishes when they close the browser window. Session cookies are created temporarily. Once you close the browser, all session cookies are deleted.
  • Third-party cookies are cookies that are set by a domain other than that of the website being visited by the user, i.e. other than www.adswizz.com. If a user visits a website and another entity sets a cookie through that website, this would be a third-party cookie.

  • Contact the 3rd PARTY PUBLISHER – You can use the mechanisms provided by the 3rd PARTY PUBLISHER to refuse the setting of the cookies, or to withdraw your consent to the cookies.
  • Use the “DO NOT TRACK” feature (which is accessible at the bottom of our Privacy Notice) – If you do not want ANY 3rd PARTY PUBLISHERS to collect information through the ADSWIZZ SERVICE COOKIE in order to display targeted advertisements, AdsWizz has implemented a tool that will prevent them from doing so. You must therefore make sure that such ADSWIZZ SERVICE COOKIES remain on your device with the DO NOT TRACK instruction set.

Why only 48 hours?

Internet Service Providers, like your broadband or cellular operator, frequently change the IP addresses they lease to their subscribers. This means that the IP address you are being leased for today could be assigned to a totally different person next day.

A DHCP-assigned IP address is not permanent and expires in about 24 hours. We have chosen to persist the opt-out preference to an IP address for 48 hours, trying to favor your privacy preference but also being careful of false-positive mitigation across other users.
